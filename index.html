An Insurance Company [again]

2.5 months, Melbourne

I ventured back into this insurance company to coach a pair of their graduates on building out a replacement test tool. The client had an application that collected SOAP XML documents from a queue and stored them in a database. A server side web application would allow filtering over the dataset. It suffered performance problems

We replaced it with an AngularJS client (shifting the HTML contruction performance to each client), a service that serves JSON documents (with hypermedia controls). The queue reader became a standalone app. This allowed us to dedicate more instances to the queues that get more activity.

The final part was profiling the components and the DB and making tweaks to get throughput up (on the queue reader) and the latency down (on the website). The thing has a hundred or so users and processes between 200,000 and 500,000 messages per day.

javascript, angularjs

An Online Advertising Company

12 months, Melbourne

I worked within a large co-sourced team to deliver a new paper-based report for advertisers. The system had four parts, a scalable Java-based event capture system. A data warehouse responsible for aggregating and organising events. A report generation process and later, an online reporting system.

My work on this project was a mixture of coaching and doing. With analysts I helped to improve how we defined the scope of each story and our acceptance critera. With developers I helped to improve the quality of their testing.

The more stereotypical work on this project was focusing on exploratory testing, user interaction testing and introducing the concept of mule testing: a technique I devised for challenging and validating assumptions in complex products in evolved domains.

ruby, sql, exporatory testing, mule testing

The Australian Federal Police

3.5 years, Canberra

I was responsible for the foundation and training of the service testing team. I created the automation framework, trained and mentored testers and helping the organisation scale from four to fifty services.

I was the Test Lead on ten projects; some of these were high profile both within and without the AFP including the NPRS (National Police Reference System) an inter-jurisdictional projects involving CrimTrac and each state's police-force. I worked on the Missing Persons project, which automated the reporting processes of the missing persons within the AFP and shared that information with NPRS. The Missing Persons project received excellent reviews from the users because it simplified their workflow and made it easier to meet their reporting obligations.

I was also responsible for building tools to assist the testing team in both testing and to facilitate testing. Such tools included an integrating Loadrunner and Quick Test Pro into a Continuous Integration server, A DOORS to Quality Centre synchronisation tool and a test data generator for performance testing.

performance testing, tool development, exploratory testing
