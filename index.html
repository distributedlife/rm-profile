The Australian Federal Police

3.5 years, Canberra

Over a period of nine months Ryan was responsible for the foundation and training of the service testing team. This involved creating an automation framework, training and mentoring testers and helping the organisation move from four to fifty services in that nine-month period.

Ryan was the Test Lead on ten projects; some of these were high profile both inside and outside the AFP. MNPP and subsequently NPRS were inter-jurisdictional projects involve CrimTrac and each state police jurisdiction. He worked on the Missing Persons project, which assisted to automate the reporting processes of the missing persons within the AFP and integrate that information into NPRS. The Missing Persons project received excellent reviews from the users because it simplified their workflow and made it easier to meet their reporting obligations.

Ryan was also responsible for building tools to assist the testing team in both testing and to facilitate testing. Such tools included an integrating Loadrunner and Quick Test Pro into a Continuous Integration server, A DOORS to Quality Centre synchronisation tool and a test data generator for performance testing.
